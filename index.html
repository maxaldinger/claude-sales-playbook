# Claude Sales Playbook — How To Get Outputs Worth Using

## The Golden Rule
The more context you give Claude, the better the output.
Two sentences of deal context beats no context every time.
Treat it like briefing a new colleague, not Googling something.

---

## Always Include When Asking Claude To Write Anything
- Who you're talking to (name, role, company, industry)
- Where you are in the deal (discovery, eval, proposal, etc.)
- What happened last (call, demo, email, objection)
- What you want this output to accomplish
- Any relevant pain, urgency, or political context you know

---

## What Claude Is Great At — Use These Daily
- Discovery call prep (pain questions, industry context, likely objections)
- Follow-up emails after any call or meeting
- MEDDIC gap analysis on a specific deal
- Building value messaging tied to business outcomes
- Mutual Action Plans from meeting notes
- Handling objections — 3 responses, different approaches
- Translating technical details into exec-friendly language
- Rewriting drafts to sound more human and less template-y
- Summarizing long email threads before jumping on a call

---

## What Claude Is NOT
- A replacement for your judgment and relationships
- Always right about pricing, terms, or product specs — verify before sending
- A way to skip discovery — AI needs context just like you do
- Guaranteed accurate on recent news or competitor info — double-check

---

## The Skills (Pre-Built Prompts — Copy, Fill In, Get Output)

| Skill | Methodology | Use When |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Discovery Prep | Sandler | Before any first or second call |
| Deal Qualifier | MEDDIC | Mid-cycle, before forecast calls |
| Value Messaging | Force Management | Building a pitch or proposal |
| Follow-Up Email | General | After any touchpoint |
| Mutual Action Plan | General | Any time you need a MAP |
| Objection Handler | General | When you're stuck on a pushback |

---

## Red Flags — Stop Before Sending
- Claude invented a specific number, date, or commitment → remove it
- Claude said something you can't verify → add [TBD] or cut it
- The tone doesn't match your relationship → humanize the opener
- It reads like a template → rewrite the first and last sentence

---

## The 10-Second Test
Read it out loud. If you wouldn't say it on a call, don't send it in an email.

RULES:
- Open with something specific from the conversation — not "great speaking with you today"
- Paragraph 1: One-line recap of what you aligned on
- Paragraph 2: Any resource, info, or commitment you said you'd send
- Paragraph 3: One clear next step with a soft CTA — don't give them multiple options
- Close warmly — match the relationship
- No buzzwords: no "synergy," "circle back," "touch base," "value-add," "solution"
- If there's real urgency, include it — don't manufacture it
- If the deal stage is late, reflect appropriate gravity without being pushy
- Under 200 words unless the situation requires more

Admin / Enablement
Setup Guide
From zero to a fully deployed team Claude Project in about two hours. No IT required.
Step 1: Fill In SOUL.md
  1. Open the SOUL.md tab in this toolkit and copy the template
  2. Fill in every section with your company's real info — voice, products, buyers, deal stages, competitors
  3. The more specific you are, the better every Claude output will be. Don't skip sections.
Step 2: Create The Claude Project
  1. Log into claude.ai — you'll need a Pro or Team account
  2. Click Projects in the left sidebar → New Project
  3. Name it something like: [Team] Sales AI Assistant
  4. Open the project → click Project Instructions
  5. Paste your completed SOUL.md into the instructions field and save
  6. Every conversation in this project will now start with your full company context
Step 3: Distribute The Playbook
  1. Copy CLAUDE.md from this toolkit
  2. Paste it into a shared doc (Notion, Confluence, SharePoint, Google Docs)
  3. Pin the link in your team Slack or Teams channel — wherever they actually look
Step 4: Run A Live Demo (20 Min)
  1. Open the Claude Project on a shared screen with your team
  2. Pick a real deal from your pipeline (anonymize if needed)
  3. Paste the Discovery Prep skill + 4-5 lines of deal context — show the output live
  4. Then do the same with Follow-Up Email using a recent call
  5. Have each rep try one skill with their own deal — live, in the room, right then
What converts fastest: The Follow-Up Email skill. Every rep has a call they need to follow up on. Show it working on a real one and they'll use it tomorrow morning. Then introduce MEDDIC once they're comfortable.
Step 5: Sustain Adoption
  1. Designate a champion — one person who collects feedback and updates skills quarterly
  2. Add skills over time — start with 2, add more as you identify repetitive writing tasks
  3. Review SOUL.md quarterly — update when products, competitors, or positioning changes
  4. Share wins in the team channel — "Claude helped me close X" normalizes the behavior
